# Core Values

The four problems LMAP exists to address.

### 1. Distribution: Democratizing Storytelling

- **Problem:** Centralized gatekeepers control which stories reach
  audiences.
- **Solution:** Permissionless distribution through IPFS with
  token-based access. Any party can build an LMAP-compatible
  storefront; the protocol does not pick winners.
- **Impact:** Any creator can distribute globally without
  intermediaries.

### 2. Preservation: Eternal Cinema

- **Problem:** Digital media is lost as platforms die and formats
  change.
- **Solution:** Content-addressed storage on IPFS. Tokens recorded on
  immutable smart contracts. LMAP works even if its founding
  stewards disappear.
- **Impact:** Movies exist forever, independent of any company or
  platform.

### 3. Protection: Honoring Creative Labor

- **Problem:** Digital media is infinitely replicable; creators are
  often uncompensated.
- **Solution:** Threshold-mediated key release with stake-based sybil
  resistance — decryption material is gated by current on-chain
  ownership at decryption time, evaluated by a distributed network
  of stake-bonded nodes. We claim, and intend to demonstrate,
  security equivalent to or stronger than legacy hardware-attested
  DRM, on an open-protocol substrate the latter structurally cannot
  match. Royalties on every transfer flow back to creators
  automatically through the protocol contract.
- **Impact:** Artists are compensated. Culture spreads naturally.
  When you give a person trust, they become trustworthy — and when
  you give honest cryptography, you don't need to treat audiences as
  adversaries.

### 4. Sovereignty: The Token, Not the Platform

- **Problem:** Streaming platforms own the relationship with
  audiences. The viewer rents access; the platform extracts the
  value.
- **Solution:** The token is the license. Tokens live on Polygon,
  not on a Wylloh-operated server. They are transferable, inheritable,
  and provably owned. LMAP is a *protocol* — like DVD, like USB —
  that any party can implement; what consumers experience is *liquid
  media*: physical-grade ownership in liquid digital form.
- **Impact:** Audiences own what they buy. The protocol outlives any
  particular platform that runs on it.
